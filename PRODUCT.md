# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Water delivery business owners and operations staff in Nepal. Primary scene: owner or ops manager at a desk or phone mid-day, juggling orders and deliveries. They need answers fast; slow UIs cost real money. Some tenants are solo owners; others have a small team where the owner configured the account and staff uses it daily. Both English and Nepali speakers, sometimes mixing within one session.

## Product Purpose

Multi-tenant SaaS platform for water jar and bottle delivery businesses in Nepal. Manages the full ops cycle: orders (B2B + B2C), customers, products, invoices (VAT 13%, IRD-compliant), driver dispatch, and inventory. Bilingual (Nepali + English). Nepali fiscal year (BS calendar) and paisa-denominated billing. This is a commercial SaaS product competing on quality against generic admin templates and spreadsheet workflows.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, fast, modern, clear. Confident without being flashy. Feels like a category-best SaaS tool (Linear, Stripe, Raycast energy) adapted for a South Asian small-business context. The UI gets out of the way and lets the work happen.

## Anti-references

- Bootstrap/jQuery-era admin templates (DataTables, AdminLTE): cluttered sidebar, too many borders, widget soup.
- Pastel "startup-cute" SaaS: excessive rounding, bouncy animations, gradient hero buttons everywhere.
- Traditional Nepal government or banking software: dated form grids, gray heavy borders, no visual hierarchy.
- Generic "water company" website aesthetic: blue gradient drops, clipart, company-brochure layouts applied to ops tools.
- SaaS dashboard monoculture: dark sidebar + white content area + teal accent + identical KPI cards in a 3-column grid.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature.** Every interaction should feel instant. No spinners where skeletons work. No modals where inline works. Density over decoration.
2. **Clarity at any density.** Tables, forms, and data must be legible when packed. Hierarchy through scale and weight, not color alone.
3. **Bilingual by default.** Nepali and English are equals. Font stacks, spacing, and layout must handle both scripts without layout shifts.
4. **Trust through precision.** This tool handles real money and real business records. Precise numbers, clear status, no ambiguity in financial data. Reliability signals matter more than delight.
5. **SaaS-grade craft.** Compete on quality. Every pixel should look like a deliberate choice, not a framework default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Older Android devices common among Nepal users; avoid heavy JS animations. Low-bandwidth scenarios: favor text over images, avoid large uncompressed assets. Color-contrast must hold in direct sunlight (mobile outdoor use is real).
